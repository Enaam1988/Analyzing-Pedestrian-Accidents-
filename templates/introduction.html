Enhancing Pedestrian Safety Through Data Analysis

Welcome to the Pedestrian Crashes (analyzing pedestrian accidents)

A comprehensive exploration of pedestrian safety and accident patterns in Australia. This project compiles and analyzes data related to pedestrian crashes reported since 2012, as documented by the Australian Federal Police (AFP) through the AFP Crash Report Form.

Project Overview and Purpose

Pedestrian safety is a critical concern, and understanding the dynamics of pedestrian accidents is paramount for developing effective safety measures and reducing fatalities. The primary purpose of this project is to provide valuable insights into pedestrian safety and accident trends across Australia. Our objectives are as follows:

  1. Analyze Crash Data: We collect, clean, and organize pedestrian crash data reported by the police or the public. By doing so, we aim to identify trends, hotspots, and contributing factors to pedestrian accidents.
  2. Enhance Safety Measures: Through data-driven insights, we strive to contribute to improved pedestrian safety measures. Our findings may aid authorities and urban planners in making informed decisions regarding road infrastructure, signage, and traffic regulations.
  3. Raise Awareness: By making this data accessible to the public and researchers, we aim to raise awareness about pedestrian safety issues. We encourage discussions and initiatives to reduce pedestrian accidents and improve road safety.

Project Importance

Pedestrian safety is a shared responsibility, and this project serves as a valuable resource for anyone interested in pedestrian safety, urban planning, or road safety initiatives in Australia. It is a starting point for data-driven analysis and discussions aimed at making our streets safer for pedestrians.
